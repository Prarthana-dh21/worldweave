Iara the River Mermaid

Iara, the River Mermaid, is a mythical figure from Tupi and Guarani legends in Brazil. Betrayed and transformed from a brave warrior into a beautiful water spirit, she now lives in rivers, symbolizing allure and sorrow.

The Curupira

Curupira is a mythical creature from Brazilian folklore who serves as a fierce guardian of the jungle. With bright red hair and backward feet that confuse hunters, he protects the forest and its animals while punishing those who exploit nature.

The Sun and the Moon

The Sun and the Moon is a Tupi and Guarani myth from Brazil, portraying a deep love between them. After being tragically separated, they now chase each other across the sky, explaining the cycle of day and night.
